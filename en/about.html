The Story

Started in a classroom,
built for the real world

I founded Korver AI Solutions in June 2025 because I kept hitting the same frustration: school projects would reach a genuinely useful point, and then stop. The scope ended, the semester moved on, and the work sat in a folder. I wanted to keep building — and to build things that actually get used.

Before getting into AI, I spent four years in customer-facing roles — customer service, business analysis, customer account management. That background matters more than it might sound. I've sat on the operations side of a business. I know what it feels like when a process is broken, when a report takes too long, when something that should be automatic isn't. That's what I build solutions for.

I'm two years into a Bachelor in Applied Data Science & AI in Breda, which keeps the technical side sharp. The rest of the time I'm here — in 's-Hertogenbosch — building, writing, and figuring out which problems are actually worth solving.

How I Work

Four things
I don't compromise on

These aren't values on a poster. They're the constraints that shape how every project gets built — whether it's a two-week automation or a months-long pipeline.

01
Understand before building

We don't open a code editor until we understand the problem, the data, and the people who will use the output. Skipping this step is how projects fail.

02
Build clean

Simple, maintainable solutions over clever ones. The measure of good engineering is whether the next person or client can understand and extend it.

03
Make it explainable

AI that stakeholders can't understand doesn't get used. Every system we build is designed to be explained. From how it works to why it made a particular decision.

04
Deliver full ownership

When a project ends, the client owns everything. Code, models, documentation. No lock-in, no dependency on us to keep the lights on.

Why One Person?

No handoffs.
No surprises.

When you work with a larger agency, your project gets scoped by one person, built by another, and handed over by a third. Things get lost. Context gets dropped. You end up explaining the same thing multiple times to people who weren't in the original conversation.

With me, that doesn't happen. I handle the discovery, the build, and the handover. Every decision gets made with the full picture in mind, because I'm the only one holding it.

That's not a limitation — for the kind of focused, practical work I do, it's exactly the right model.
