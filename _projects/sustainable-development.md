## Overview

Academic researchers spend a disproportionate amount of time on literature review — searching, reading, and synthesising papers before they can even begin their own work. This project set out to reduce that burden significantly.

## The Challenge

The core problem wasn't finding papers — tools like Google Scholar handle that well enough. The challenge was making sense of large sets of papers quickly: understanding how they relate to each other, identifying consensus and disagreement, and surfacing the most relevant findings.

## What We Built

A web application that takes a research question as input, retrieves relevant papers, and generates a structured synthesis — grouping findings by theme, highlighting contradictions, and flagging gaps in the literature. Researchers can drill into any section to see the source papers.

## Outcome

Early users reported cutting their literature review time in half. The tool is now being used across multiple research groups and continues to be refined based on feedback.
